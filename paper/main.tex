\begin{abstract}
\noindent
% ABSTRACT PLACEHOLDER
We classify four-dimensional $\N=1$ asymptotically free $\SU(N)\times\SU(N)$
quiver gauge theories admitting a non-trivial large-$N$ limit without Veneziano
scaling of fundamental matter, and analyse the superconformal fixed points and
renormalisation-group flow phases that arise in the two-coupling space.
Seven universality classes are found, each saturating $a=c$ to leading order
in $1/N$. Applying the boundary-analysis prescription of
Intriligator--Wecht (\texttt{hep-th/0502049}) to both decoupled single-node
fixed points, we classify each class by the sign pattern of the boundary
functions $\Bcond$ and compute the flow topology in the $(g_{0},g_{1})$-plane.
As our main result, we exhibit an explicit realisation of the
``both boundaries IR-stable'' topology (Figure~6 of \cite{IW2005}), which
those authors noted had no known examples: deforming class~16 by adding $\Nf$
fundamentals on a single node in the window
$0.427\,N-1<\Nf<N-1$ yields precisely this phase.
\end{abstract}
