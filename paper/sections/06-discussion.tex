\subsection{Summary}

We have classified all four-dimensional $\N=1$ asymptotically free
$\SU(N)\times\SU(N)$ quiver gauge theories in the non-Veneziano
large-$N$ limit, using symbolic $a$-maximisation to compute IR
R-charges and central charges.  Seven universality classes arise.  All
five non-degenerate classes exhibit $a=c$ to leading order in $1/N$,
reflecting a common enhancement of the extremal R-symmetry to a
particular flavour subgroup.

Applying the Intriligator--Wecht boundary prescription
\cite{IW2005} to both decoupled single-node limits, we have computed
$\Bcond_{A}$ and $\Bcond_{B}$ for every class and classified each by
the sign pattern of the pair $(\Bcond_{A},\Bcond_{B})$.  Class $2$
realises the Figure~5 topology (interior SCFT is the global IR
attractor); classes $4,16,21,23$ realise a mixed topology in which
a single axis SCFT attracts generic flows.

Our main result is that the strictly non-Veneziano landscape misses
the Figure~6 topology of \cite{IW2005}, but that a single-parameter
(Veneziano) deformation of class~$16$ realises it:
adding $\Nf$ fundamental pairs to node~$1$ in the window
$0.427\,N-1<\Nf<N-1$ produces $(\Bcond_{A},\Bcond_{B})=(+,+)$, i.e.\
both axis SCFTs IR-stable.  The interior R-charge extremum persists
but ceases to be an attractor; flows split between the two axis
SCFTs through a separatrix.  We are not aware of a previous explicit
realisation of this topology.

\subsection{Implications}

\paragraph{$a=c$ in the equal-rank non-Veneziano sector.}  The
systematic appearance of $a=c$ across all five interacting classes
suggests that the non-Veneziano restriction is a selective filter:
most generic two-node SCFTs in the full database have $a\neq c$ at
leading $N$, and it is the absence of $\mathcal O(N)$-many fundamentals
that effectively preserves the relevant R-symmetry enhancement.
Quantifying this (e.g.\ by counting the $a=c$ fraction as a function
of the Veneziano parameter $x$) is a natural next step.

\paragraph{Holographic dual for Figure~6?}  In $\cite{IW2005}$ the
Figure~5 topology was matched to a gravity dual in which two gauge
factors on D-branes realise the interior SCFT.  A Figure~6 phase has
two possible axis IR attractors and is therefore dual to a bulk
geometry with two distinct IR asymptotics.  Whether the deformed
class~$16$ admits such a holographic description --- and whether the
basin-separatrix structure admits an interpretation in the dual ---
is an open question.

\paragraph{Persistence beyond one loop.}  The boundary criterion we
have used is strictly one-loop (via NSVZ) and linear in the
bifundamental anomalous dimension; two- and higher-loop corrections
can shift $\Bcond$-thresholds by $\mathcal O(1/N)$ amounts.  The
lower edge of the Figure~6 window at $\Nf=0.427\,N-1$ is therefore
expected to receive $1/N$ corrections; the upper edge $\Nf=N-1$ is the
UV-AF cliff and is exact at one loop.  Because $\Bcond_{B}$ is
$\mathcal O(N)$ and of definite sign throughout the window, the
robustness of the Figure~6 phase against higher-loop corrections is
on firm footing.

\subsection{Future work}
\label{sec:future}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{$n$-node quivers.}  Extending the present analysis to
    linear and cyclic quivers with $n\ge 3$ nodes requires a
    $2^{n}$-fold sign-pattern analysis on the boundaries; we expect
    richer topologies including partial basins and sub-quiver
    attractors.
  \item \textbf{$\SO/\Sp$ sectors.}  The database also contains
    $\SU$--$\SO$, $\SU$--$\Sp$, and $\SO$--$\Sp$ two-node theories.
    A parallel analysis of these mixed-group sectors in the
    non-Veneziano limit should uncover additional realisations of the
    three topologies.
  \item \textbf{Explicit RG flow numerics.}  Integrating the NSVZ
    flow equations in the $(g_{0},g_{1})$ plane for class~$16$ +
    $\Nf$ on node~$1$ --- with R-charges updated self-consistently
    along the trajectory --- would make the separatrix and basin
    structure manifest.  This is a numerically tractable problem
    given the a-maximisation infrastructure already in place.
  \item \textbf{Veneziano-parameter scan.}  The transition
    $\Bcond_{A}<0\to\Bcond_{A}>0$ at $\Nf=0.427\,N-1$ is a phase
    boundary of the two-coupling RG topology.  A full characterisation
    of the phase diagram in $(x^{(0)},x^{(1)})$-space (with $x^{(a)}$
    the Veneziano ratio at each node) would generalise the present
    results to the Veneziano regime.
\end{itemize}
