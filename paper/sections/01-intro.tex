Four-dimensional $\N=1$ supersymmetric gauge theories provide one of
the richest arenas for exact non-perturbative statements about
renormalisation-group flow.  For a single gauge group, the $a$-theorem
\cite{KomargodskiSchwimmer}, the conformal window of SQCD
\cite{Seiberg1994}, and the identification of the superconformal
$R$-symmetry by $a$-maximisation \cite{IntriligatorWecht2003} together
give a remarkably complete picture of the space of IR superconformal
fixed points.  Moving from a single gauge group to a quiver of several
gauge factors brings two new features of immediate physical interest.
First, the infra-red is no longer a single point but in general a
continuous family of superconformal theories labelled by the exactly
marginal ratios of the gauge couplings of the individual factors.
Second, the two-loop space between the ultraviolet Gaussian fixed
point and the available infra-red fixed points organises itself into
distinct \emph{RG-flow topologies}, characterised by which fixed
points are attractors, which are saddles, and how basins of attraction
are arranged.

For a two-node quiver with gauge group
$\SU(N)_{1}\times\SU(N)_{2}$ and coupling plane
$(g_{1},g_{2})\in\mathbb R_{\ge 0}^{2}$, the candidate IR fixed points
are the Gaussian origin, the two single-node (``axis'') SCFTs obtained
by decoupling one gauge factor, and any interior two-coupling SCFT
that may arise.  A central question is:
\emph{which axis and interior fixed points attract generic flows,
and under what conditions does the interior SCFT emerge as the
global IR attractor?}
The answer depends on the signs of two one-loop coefficients
controlling the stability of each axis SCFT against turning on the
orthogonal coupling.  Following Intriligator and
Wecht~\cite{IW2005}, these stability coefficients are computed by
$a$-maximising the decoupled single-node sub-theory and substituting
the resulting bifundamental R-charge into an effective one-loop
formula; the result is a leading-$N$ quantity of the schematic form
$b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$, representing the one-loop coefficient
of node $a$ when node $b\ne a$ sits at its axis SCFT.

\cite{IW2005} identified three RG-flow topologies distinguished by
the sign pattern $(\text{sign}\,b_{0}^{(1)}(g_{2}^{\star}),
\text{sign}\,b_{0}^{(2)}(g_{1}^{\star}))$: both positive (Figure~5 of
\cite{IW2005}: both axes are IR-unstable, the interior SCFT is the
global IR attractor); mixed (one positive, one negative: one axis is
the attractor and the interior is a saddle); and both negative
(Figure~6 of \cite{IW2005}: both axes are IR-stable, generic flows
are caught by one of them through a separatrix through the interior).
The authors of \cite{IW2005} reported that examples of the first two
topologies were straightforward to construct, but that they did not
find an explicit realisation of the third, both-axes-stable topology.

\medskip
In the present paper, we classify the equal-rank
$\SU(N)\times\SU(N)$ quiver gauge theories that admit a large-$N$
limit \emph{without} Veneziano scaling of fundamental matter (i.e.\
$\Nf$ of order $N^{0}$, not $\mathcal O(N)$); compute the boundary
one-loop coefficients $b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ for every
universality class that emerges; tabulate the RG-flow topology of
each class; and exhibit explicitly the Figure~6 topology by a simple
single-parameter Veneziano deformation of one of the classes.

\paragraph{Main results.}

\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{3pt}
  \item \textbf{Classification.}  Nine equal-rank non-Veneziano
    universality classes of $\SU(N)\times\SU(N)$ arise from a
    systematic enumeration (Section~\ref{sec:classification},
    Table~\ref{tab:nine-classes}).  Every class with a defined
    leading-$N$ $a$-maximum has $a=c$ to leading order in $1/N$.
    Multiple distinct quiver representatives inhabit each class
    (Table~\ref{tab:class-reps}), demonstrating that each is a
    genuine universality class rather than a relabelling.

  \item \textbf{Phase distribution.}  The nine classes split into
    one genuine Figure~5-phase class (class~2), four mixed-phase
    classes (classes $4,16,21,23$), one leading-$N$ marginal class
    (class~44), two below-unitarity classes formally in the Figure~5
    bin (classes~$1$ and~$9$), and a \emph{nonSCFT} umbrella grouping
    class~$877$ (below the SQCD conformal window at its empty node)
    with 20 flat-direction theories carrying no class label.  No
    strict non-Veneziano equal-rank class realises the Figure~6
    topology.

  \item \textbf{Figure~6 realisation.}  Deforming the representative
    of class~16 by $\Nf^{(2)}$ fundamentals on node~2 in the window
    \begin{equation}
      0.427\,N-1\;<\;\Nf^{(2)}\;<\;N-1
      \label{eq:main-window}
    \end{equation}
    yields the Figure~6 topology with both axis SCFTs IR-stable,
    the interior $a$-max a saddle, and a separatrix dividing the
    two basins of attraction (Section~\ref{sec:phases-class16}).  For
    $N\ge 4$ this window is non-empty; its width grows linearly with
    $N$ at $\approx 0.573\,N$.  The phase transition at the lower
    edge is the flipping of one boundary coefficient's sign as
    $\Nf^{(2)}$ crosses a leading-$N$ threshold.  This is the first
    explicit realisation of \cite[Fig.~6]{IW2005} of which we are
    aware.
\end{enumerate}

The essential physical mechanism is simple: the boundary analysis of
\cite{IW2005} uses the anomalous dimension of the bifundamental at
the axis SCFT to correct the one-loop coefficient of the decoupled
coupling; adding free fundamentals on a single node raises the
bifundamental R-charge on the corresponding boundary (through the
decoupled-sub-theory $a$-maximisation) and thereby reduces the
magnitude of the bifundamental anomalous-dimension correction, with
quantitative consequences for the sign pattern.  A class whose positive
boundary coefficient is small enough in absolute terms (class~16) is
the one for which the sign-flip threshold sits inside the UV-AF
window of the deformed node; classes whose positive boundary
coefficient is large (class~4) cannot be pushed into Figure~6 by a
single-parameter deformation.

\medskip
The rest of the paper is organised as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:setup} sets up the class of theories, the
non-Veneziano large-$N$ limit, the enumeration cap $k_{\text{tot}}\le
4$, and the boundary one-loop coefficient
$b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ that we use throughout.
Section~\ref{sec:classification} presents the enumeration and
reports the nine universality classes with their central charges,
multiple representatives, and exact R-charges.
Section~\ref{sec:boundary} tabulates the boundary coefficients for
all classes with a defined leading-$N$ $a$-maximum and assigns each a
sign pattern.
Section~\ref{sec:phases} gives the class-by-class RG-flow topology
and presents the Figure~6 realisation (the main result).
Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses implications, open directions
and caveats.  Appendix~\ref{app:mixed} collects the analogous data
for mixed-rank $\SU(m_{1}N)\times\SU(m_{2}N)$ classes.
