\subsection{The corrected AF budget at each boundary}
\label{sec:boundary-corrected}

Let $\alpha^{(a)}_{0}$ denote the leading-$N$ coefficient of the
one-loop beta function at node~$a$, $b_{0}^{(a)}(N)=\alpha^{(a)}_{0}\,N
+\gamma^{(a)}_{0}$.  In the UV the AF condition is $b_{0}^{(a)}>0$.
At the boundary where the opposite node is interacting at its own IR
fixed point, the bifundamental fields acquire anomalous dimensions that
shift the effective budget.  Explicitly, evaluating $\Bcond_{a}$ from
\eqref{eq:Bcond-def} using the a-maximised bifundamental R-charges,
one finds that it can be written in the same linear form
\begin{equation}
  \Bcond_{a}\bigl(\Nf^{(a)}\bigr)\;=\;\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}\,N
  +\gamma^{(a)}_{\Bcond}\;-\;\tfrac{1}{2}\,\Nf^{(a)}\,,
  \label{eq:Bcond-linear}
\end{equation}
with the correction to the AF slope equal to
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;\Delta\alpha^{(a)}\;=\;\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}-\alpha^{(a)}_{0}
  \;=\;N_{\text{bif}}\,
        T_{\text{fund}}(G_{a}^{\text{free}})
        \,\dim_{\text{lead}}(G_{a}^{\text{active}})
        \,m_{\text{active}}
        \,\bigl(3R_{\text{bif}}^{(a)}-2\bigr)\;}
  \label{eq:delta-alpha}
\end{equation}
where $N_{\text{bif}}$ is the number of bifundamental edges,
$m_{\text{active}}$ is the rank multiplier of the active node, and
$R_{\text{bif}}^{(a)}$ is the a-maximised R-charge of the bifundamental
at boundary~$a$.  The sign of $\Delta\alpha^{(a)}$ thus tracks the sign
of $3R_{\text{bif}}-2$, i.e.\ the sign of the bifundamental
anomalous dimension.

The stored quantity in our classification database is the
\emph{corrected UV-AF bound}
\begin{equation}
  \Nf^{(a)}\;\le\;\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}\,N
  +2\gamma^{(a)}_{\Bcond}\,,
  \label{eq:Bcond-ineq}
\end{equation}
the equation $\Nf^{(a)}=\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}N+2\gamma^{(a)}_{\Bcond}$
being precisely $\Bcond_{a}=0$.  The $\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}$-coefficient
is, up to a positive constant, the NSVZ numerator at the opposite
boundary; in particular \textbf{$\alpha_{\Bcond}^{(a)}>0\Leftrightarrow
\Tr[R^{(a)}G_{a}^{2}]>0\Leftrightarrow\Bcond_{a}<0\Leftrightarrow$
axis $a$ is IR-unstable}.

\paragraph{Reading the table.}
The $\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}$-coefficient of the B-condition is the
leading-$N$ slope of $\Bcond_{a}$ as a function of $\Nf^{(a)}$ added to
the \emph{free} node~$a$.  At $\Nf^{(a)}=0$ (non-Veneziano limit),
the sign of $\alpha^{(a)}_{\Bcond}$ alone determines whether boundary
$a$ is IR-unstable ($\alpha_{\Bcond}>0$) or IR-stable
($\alpha_{\Bcond}<0$) in the large-$N$ limit.

\subsection{The three RG topologies and their incidence}
\label{sec:boundary-phases}

Across the five interacting classes the sign pattern of
$(\alpha_{\Bcond,A},\alpha_{\Bcond,B})$ splits into exactly two
topologies:

\begin{center}
  \begin{tabular}{lc}
    \toprule
    Phase & Classes \\
    \midrule
    Fig.~5 (both axes IR-unstable; interior SCFT is global attractor) &
    $\{2\}$\\
    Mixed (one axis IR-unstable, one IR-stable) &
    $\{4,16,21,23\}$\\
    Fig.~6 (both axes IR-stable; interior SCFT is not an attractor) &
    \emph{none at $\Nf=0$}\\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{center}

Thus, in the strict non-Veneziano limit, the classified
$\SU(N)\times\SU(N)$ landscape contains both the
$\cite[\text{Fig.~5}]{IW2005}$ case and the mixed topology, but
\emph{none} of the $\cite[\text{Fig.~6}]{IW2005}$ case.  This agrees
qualitatively with the remark of \cite{IW2005} that Figure~6
topologies were not found in their examples.

Section~\ref{sec:phases} will show, however, that a small-parameter
deformation of class~$16$ --- adding $\Nf$ fundamentals on node~$1$
in the window \eqref{eq:main-window} --- flips the sign of
$\alpha_{\Bcond,A}$ while preserving the sign of $\alpha_{\Bcond,B}$,
realising the Figure~6 topology explicitly.

\subsection{A caveat on the bifundamental R-charge}
\label{sec:boundary-caveat}

A point of care: the strictly proper boundary prescription of
\cite{IW2005} computes the bifundamental R-charges by
$a$-maximising the \emph{sub-quiver} at each boundary (with the
bifundamentals promoted to flavour fields of the single active node),
rather than using the interior fixed-point R-charge.  The two
prescriptions generally disagree because the sub-quiver is a
different CFT from the full two-node one.  In Table~\ref{tab:Bcond}
we have reported the $\alpha_{\Bcond}$ coefficients as obtained from
the pipeline of our classification database, which uses the interior
$R_{\text{bif}}$ value as a proxy for the boundary R-charge;
equivalently, the $\alpha_{\Bcond}$ we report is the sign of the
NSVZ numerator at the \emph{interior} IR fixed point evaluated at
$g_{\text{free}}=0$.  Both quantities share the sign of $3R_{\text{bif}}-2$
under the mild assumption that neither prescription has $R_{\text{bif}}$
cross the free-field value $2/3$.  We adopt this assumption throughout
and note that the ordering of the non-Veneziano classes in
Table~\ref{tab:Bcond} is robust against the approximation; the
quantitative threshold $\Nf=0.427\,N-1$ in the class-$16$
Figure~6 window is however subject to $\mathcal O(1/N)$ corrections
when the proper boundary sub-quiver $a$-maximisation is used.
