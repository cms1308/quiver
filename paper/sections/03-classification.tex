\subsection{Enumeration pipeline}
\label{sec:class-enumeration}

We enumerate the equal-rank non-Veneziano sector as follows.  Fix the
bifundamental multiplicity vector
$k=(k_{++},k_{--},k_{+-},k_{-+})$ with total
$k_{\text{tot}}\le 4$ (cf.~Section~\ref{sec:setup-largeN}) and, for
each such $k$, scan the allowed tensor multiplicity tuples
$(n_{\adj}^{(a)},n_{S}^{(a)},n_{\bar S}^{(a)},n_{A}^{(a)},n_{\bar A}^{(a)})$
at each node subject to:
\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
  \item the leading-$N$ asymptotic-freedom condition
    \eqref{eq:AF-lead} at each node;
  \item the cubic-anomaly cancellation \eqref{eq:anomaly}
    \emph{exactly} (i.e.\ identically in $N$), enforced as the two
    separate equations \eqref{eq:anomaly-N}
    and~\eqref{eq:anomaly-const}.  In the non-Veneziano sector
    \eqref{eq:anomaly-N-nonVen} fixes an integer linear relation
    between the bifundamental chiral excess and the tensor
    multiplicities at each node, and the remaining
    $\mathcal O(N^{0})$ discrepancy in~\eqref{eq:anomaly-const} is
    absorbed by a finite chiral excess of fundamentals
    $\Nf^{(a),+}-\Nf^{(a),-}\in\mathbb Z$;
  \item discrete symmetries exchanging the two nodes, reflecting
    bifundamentals $(\fund\leftrightarrow\afund)$ at each end
    independently, and orienting the bifundamental edges.
\end{enumerate}
The enumeration is implemented in \texttt{quiver\_generation.py}, in
which the function \texttt{chiral\_excess\_coeffs(\ldots)} returns
the coefficients $(a_{N},a_{0})$ such that the required chiral
excess is $\Nf^{(a),+}-\Nf^{(a),-}=a_{N}\,N+a_{0}$; in the
non-Veneziano sector the scan is restricted to quivers with
$a_{N}=0$, so the excess is $\mathcal O(N^{0})$ as demanded by the
non-Veneziano definition.
Each surviving theory is then subjected to symbolic
$a$-maximisation: the R-charge manifold is parameterised by
\eqref{eq:anomaly-free}, $a(R)$ of \eqref{eq:a-trial} is extremised
symbolically, and only critical points inside the allowed polytope
with negative-definite Hessian are retained.  Theories sharing the
same value of $a/N^{2}$ (to $10^{-10}$ numerical precision after
algebraic simplification) are grouped into a universality
class.\footnote{We use the class labels of the global $135$-class
table of the full database without relabelling.}  A universality class
may contain theories with genuinely distinct matter and/or edge
structure, as illustrated below.

\paragraph{Flat-direction exclusion.}
A small number of quivers admit a \emph{flat direction} at the
critical point of the $a$-trial: the Hessian has a zero eigenvalue,
so the critical point is not a genuine local maximum and the IR
R-charges are not uniquely determined.  These theories correspond to
moduli spaces of near-IR R-symmetries rather than to isolated SCFT
fixed points, and the leading-$N$ value of $a$ along the flat
direction is not a well-defined invariant of the class.  Concretely,
20 non-Veneziano theories in the enumeration exhibit such a flat
direction at leading~$N$ — all of them are empty-node quivers in
which one gauge node carries only bifundamental matter and the other
carries a pair of distinct rank-2 tensors with equal multiplicity
(e.g.\ $S+\bar S$ or $A+\bar A$); the leading-$N$ anomaly-free
conditions then conspire to pin $R_{\text{tensor}_1}+R_{\text{tensor}_2}=2$,
making the $a$-trial independent of the difference
$R_{\text{tensor}_1}-R_{\text{tensor}_2}$.  We exclude these 20
degenerate theories from all class counts below; the remaining
$432-20=412$ non-Veneziano theories distribute among the nine classes
summarised in Table~\ref{tab:nine-classes}.

\paragraph{Remark on $a=c$.}
Every class in Table~\ref{tab:nine-classes} with a defined leading-$N$
central charge satisfies $a/c=1$ at leading order in $1/N$ (i.e.\
$\Tr R^{\star}=\Tr R^{\star 3}$ modulo $\mathcal O(N^{0})$ terms).
This is a direct consequence of the absence of Veneziano fundamentals:
it is the $\mathcal O(N)$-many fundamentals that would contribute the
asymmetric term breaking $a=c$ at leading $N$.

\subsection{Four special classes: 1, 9, 44, and 877}
\label{sec:class-special}

Four classes deserve separate comment because their R-charge
assignment sits on the boundary of the physically allowed polytope,
or because the $a$-maximisation does not return a finite interior
critical point.

\paragraph{Class 1 ($a/N^{2}=0$, $R_{\text{bif}}=0$).}
Symbolic $a$-maximisation on class 1 returns an extremum at the
corner $R_{\bar A}^{(1)}=R_{\bar A}^{(2)}=R_{\text{bif}}=0$, which
yields $a/N^{2}=0$.  Because the scalar chiral primary of the
gauge-invariant operator $\Phi_{\text{bif}}\Phi_{\text{bif}}^{\dagger}$
has $\Delta=3R_{\text{bif}}/2=0$, well below the unitarity bound
$\Delta\ge 1$ (and in fact below the bound $\Delta\ge 1/2$ for
scalars), the leading-$N$ $a$-maximum does not describe a unitary
interacting SCFT.  In practice, the true IR of a theory in this class
is either a free theory in the far infra-red, or a non-trivial fixed
point outside the leading-$N$ R-symmetry parameter space used by
$a$-max; either way, the leading-$N$ analysis of the decoupled
sub-theory and the resulting boundary coefficient
$b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ are still \emph{well-defined} quantities
and are included in the phase analysis of
Section~\ref{sec:boundary}.  We flag class 1 as non-interacting at
leading $N$ and treat it on the same footing as the other classes
for the RG-topology discussion.

\paragraph{Class 9 ($a/N^{2}=27/64$, $R_{\text{bif}}=1/2$).}
Symbolic $a$-maximisation on class 9 returns the symmetric critical
point $R_{\text{bif}}=R_{\text{tensor}}^{(1,2)}=1/2$, giving
$a/N^{2}=27/64$.  Every matter field has anomalous dimension
$\gamma=3R-2=-1/2$ at this point.  As with class~1, the resulting
R-charge falls below the unitarity bound $R\ge 2/3$ required for a
gauge-invariant chiral composite to satisfy $\Delta\ge 1$: the scalar
chiral primary $\Phi_{\text{bif}}\Phi_{\text{bif}}^{\dagger}$ has
$\Delta=3R_{\text{bif}}/2=3/4<1$, violating unitarity.  Class 9 is
therefore the \emph{second} non-unitary corner class (alongside
class 1) of the leading-$N$ enumeration, lying at the intermediate
value $R_{\text{bif}}=1/2$ rather than at the extreme corner $R=0$.
The boundary coefficients $b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ remain
well-defined leading-$N$ quantities and are reported in
Section~\ref{sec:boundary}; as with class 1, the Figure-5 phase
assignment that emerges should be interpreted as a leading-$N$ formal
sign pattern rather than as a claim about the physical IR.

\paragraph{Class 44 ($a/N^{2}=1/2$, $R_{\text{bif}}=2/3$).}
The $a$-maximal R-charges of class 44 equal the free-field value
$2/3$, giving $a/N^{2}=\dim(\adj)/N^{2}\cdot\tfrac{2}{3}\cdot\tfrac{3}{2}/\ldots = 1/2$.
Equivalently: on each node the matter content exactly saturates the
leading-$N$ AF bound,
$\alpha^{(a)}=3-n_{\adj}^{(a)}-\tfrac{1}{2}(n_{S}+n_{\bar S}+n_{A}+n_{\bar A})
-\tfrac{k_{\text{tot}}}{2}=0$, so that at leading $N$ the one-loop
beta function vanishes and the anomalous dimensions that would
normally drive the theory to a non-trivial R-charge are absent.  This
class is therefore leading-$N$ \emph{marginal}: $b_{0}^{(a)}=0$ at
both nodes to leading order, with subleading $\gamma^{(a)}$-corrections
that may be positive or negative depending on the representative.  As
a result the boundary coefficients $b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ also
vanish at leading $N$, and the RG topology of the class is determined
by the sub-leading structure.  We include class 44 in
Section~\ref{sec:boundary} with this leading-order marginality made
explicit.

\paragraph{Class 877 ($a/N^{2}=0$; empty-node class).}
Class 877 collects quivers in which one of the two nodes has no
intrinsic matter ($n_{\adj}^{(a)}=n_{S}^{(a)}=n_{\bar S}^{(a)}=
n_{A}^{(a)}=n_{\bar A}^{(a)}=0$ for $a=1$ or $a=2$), so the entire
matter content of that node consists of bifundamentals to the other
node.  Of the 24 non-Veneziano empty-node enumerants, 15 have a flat
direction at the leading-$N$ $a$-maximisation and are excluded under
the criterion of Section~\ref{sec:class-enumeration}; the remaining 9
admit a unique interior critical point at $a/N^{2}=0$, with all
matter R-charges on the unitarity corner of the polytope.  These 9
representatives share the qualitative features of class 1 — vanishing
leading-$N$ central charge, R-charges on the boundary of the
unitarity polytope — but are morphologically distinct (one empty
node).  As in class~1 the leading-$N$ $a$-maximum does not describe a
unitary interacting SCFT; the boundary coefficients
$b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ nevertheless remain well-defined
leading-$N$ quantities and are included in the phase analysis of
Section~\ref{sec:boundary}.

\paragraph{Summary.}
Classes $2,4,16,21,23$ have interior R-charge extrema within the
unitarity polytope; classes $1$ and $9$ have their extrema on (or
below) the unitarity boundary; class $44$ saturates the free-field
boundary at leading $N$; class $877$ collects the 9 empty-node
representatives with a unique leading-$N$ $a$-maximum at $a/N^{2}=0$
and all surviving R-charges vanishing, qualitatively matching class~1
but morphologically distinct.  The eight classes with a defined
leading-$N$ sign pattern $(1,2,4,9,16,21,23,44)$ are retained in the
RG-flow topology analysis of
Sections~\ref{sec:boundary}--\ref{sec:phases}; class~877 is omitted
from the boundary-coefficient tables because its morphology (one
empty node) places the entire matter sector on bifundamental
operators, for which the sub-quiver decoupling limit requires a
separate analysis.
