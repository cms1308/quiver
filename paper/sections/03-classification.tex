\subsection{Enumeration pipeline}
\label{sec:class-enumeration}

We enumerate the equal-rank non-Veneziano sector as follows.  Fix the
bifundamental multiplicity vector
$k=(k_{++},k_{--},k_{+-},k_{-+})$ with total
$k_{\text{tot}}\le 4$ (cf.~Section~\ref{sec:setup-largeN}) and, for
each such $k$, scan the allowed tensor multiplicity tuples
$(n_{\adj}^{(a)},n_{S}^{(a)},n_{\bar S}^{(a)},n_{A}^{(a)},n_{\bar A}^{(a)})$
at each node subject to:
\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
  \item the leading-$N$ asymptotic-freedom condition
    \eqref{eq:AF-lead} at each node;
  \item the cubic-anomaly cancellation \eqref{eq:anomaly}
    \emph{exactly} (i.e.\ identically in $N$), enforced as the two
    separate equations \eqref{eq:anomaly-N}
    and~\eqref{eq:anomaly-const}.  In the non-Veneziano sector
    \eqref{eq:anomaly-N-nonVen} fixes an integer linear relation
    between the bifundamental chiral excess and the tensor
    multiplicities at each node, and the remaining
    $\mathcal O(N^{0})$ discrepancy in~\eqref{eq:anomaly-const} is
    absorbed by a finite chiral excess of fundamentals
    $\Nf^{(a),+}-\Nf^{(a),-}\in\mathbb Z$;
  \item discrete symmetries exchanging the two nodes, reflecting
    bifundamentals $(\fund\leftrightarrow\afund)$ at each end
    independently, and orienting the bifundamental edges.
\end{enumerate}
The enumeration is implemented in \texttt{quiver\_generation.py}, in
which the function \texttt{chiral\_excess\_coeffs(\ldots)} returns
the coefficients $(a_{N},a_{0})$ such that the required chiral
excess is $\Nf^{(a),+}-\Nf^{(a),-}=a_{N}\,N+a_{0}$; in the
non-Veneziano sector the scan is restricted to quivers with
$a_{N}=0$, so the excess is $\mathcal O(N^{0})$ as demanded by the
non-Veneziano definition.
Each surviving theory is then subjected to symbolic
$a$-maximisation: the R-charge manifold is parameterised by
\eqref{eq:anomaly-free}, $a(R)$ of \eqref{eq:a-trial} is extremised
symbolically, and only critical points inside the allowed polytope
with negative-definite Hessian are retained.  Theories sharing the
same value of $a/N^{2}$ (to $10^{-10}$ numerical precision after
algebraic simplification) are grouped into a universality
class.\footnote{We use the class labels of the global $135$-class
table of the full database without relabelling.}  A universality class
may contain theories with genuinely distinct matter and/or edge
structure, as illustrated below.

\paragraph{Flat-direction (nonSCFT) theories.}
A small number of quivers admit a \emph{flat direction} at the
critical point of the $a$-trial: the Hessian has a zero eigenvalue,
so the critical point is not a genuine local maximum and the IR
R-charges are not uniquely determined.  These theories correspond to
moduli spaces of near-IR R-symmetries rather than to isolated SCFT
fixed points, and the leading-$N$ value of $a$ along the flat
direction is not a well-defined invariant of the class.  Concretely,
20 non-Veneziano theories in the enumeration exhibit such a flat
direction at leading~$N$ — all of them are empty-node quivers in
which one gauge node carries only bifundamental matter and the other
carries a pair of distinct rank-2 tensors with equal multiplicity
(e.g.\ $S+\bar S$ or $A+\bar A$); the leading-$N$ anomaly-free
conditions then conspire to pin $R_{\text{tensor}_1}+R_{\text{tensor}_2}=2$,
making the $a$-trial independent of the difference
$R_{\text{tensor}_1}-R_{\text{tensor}_2}$.  These 20 theories are
assigned to the \emph{nonSCFT} umbrella introduced below (together
with class~$877$, whose representatives lie below the SQCD conformal
window and therefore do not flow to a unitary interacting SCFT at
leading~$N$) and are excluded from the nine-class tabulation; the
remaining $432-20=412$ non-Veneziano theories distribute among the
nine classes summarised in Table~\ref{tab:nine-classes}.

\paragraph{Remark on $a=c$.}
Every class in Table~\ref{tab:nine-classes} with a defined leading-$N$
central charge satisfies $a/c=1$ at leading order in $1/N$ (i.e.\
$\Tr R^{\star}=\Tr R^{\star 3}$ modulo $\mathcal O(N^{0})$ terms).
This is a direct consequence of the absence of Veneziano fundamentals:
it is the $\mathcal O(N)$-many fundamentals that would contribute the
asymmetric term breaking $a=c$ at leading $N$.

\subsection{Special classes: 1, 9, 44, and the nonSCFT umbrella (877 + flat-direction)}
\label{sec:class-special}

Five classes deserve separate comment: the below-unitarity classes
$1$ and $9$, the leading-$N$ marginal class $44$, and the
\emph{nonSCFT} umbrella grouping class $877$ with the 20 flat-direction
theories of Section~\ref{sec:class-enumeration}.  The nonSCFT umbrella
collects those representatives that do not flow to a unitary
interacting IR SCFT at leading~$N$ because either (i)~the
leading-$N$ $a$-maximisation does not identify a genuine local
maximum (flat-direction theories), or (ii)~at least one gauge node
has no rank-2 tensor matter and carries too few effective flavours to
sit inside the SQCD conformal window, following the criterion
$x^{(a)}=N_{\text{bif}}\,T_{\text{lead}}^{(a,b)}\,m_{b}/m_{a}<3/2$
implemented in \texttt{two\_node\_db.py}; in the equal-rank
non-Veneziano scope this latter criterion is satisfied exactly by
the 9 surviving representatives of class $877$.

\paragraph{Class 1 ($a/N^{2}=0$, $R_{\text{bif}}=0$; below unitarity).}
Symbolic $a$-maximisation on class 1 returns an extremum at the
corner $R_{\bar A}^{(1)}=R_{\bar A}^{(2)}=R_{\text{bif}}=0$, which
yields $a/N^{2}=0$.  Because a chiral primary with $R=0$ would have
$\Delta=3R/2=0$, well below the unitarity bound $\Delta\ge 1$ for
gauge-invariant chiral composites in an interacting SCFT, the
leading-$N$ $a$-maximum does not describe a unitary interacting SCFT.
In practice the true IR of a theory in this class is either a free
theory, or a non-trivial fixed point outside the leading-$N$
R-symmetry parameter space (for instance through Kutasov–Schwimmer
decoupling of the offending operators); either way, the
leading-$N$ analysis of the decoupled sub-theory and the resulting
boundary coefficient $b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ remain
\emph{well-defined} quantities and are included in the phase analysis
of Section~\ref{sec:boundary}.  We flag class~1 as non-interacting at
leading~$N$ and treat it on the same footing as the other classes for
the RG-topology discussion, with the caveat that the Figure-5
assignment it receives is a formal leading-$N$ sign pattern.

\paragraph{Class 9 ($a/N^{2}=27/64$, $R_{\text{bif}}=1/2$; below unitarity).}
Symbolic $a$-maximisation on class 9 returns the symmetric critical
point $R_{\text{bif}}=R_{\text{tensor}}^{(1,2)}=1/2$, giving
$a/N^{2}=27/64$.  Every matter field has anomalous dimension
$\gamma=3R-2=-1/2$ at this point.  As with class~1, the resulting
R-charge falls below the unitarity threshold $R\ge 2/3$ required for
a gauge-invariant chiral composite to satisfy $\Delta\ge 1$, so the
leading-$N$ $a$-max does not describe a unitary interacting SCFT.
Class~9 is therefore the second below-unitarity class (alongside
class~1), lying at the intermediate value $R_{\text{bif}}=1/2$ rather
than at the extreme corner $R=0$.  The boundary coefficients
$b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ remain well-defined and are reported in
Section~\ref{sec:boundary}; as with class~1, the Figure-5 phase
assignment that emerges is a formal leading-$N$ sign pattern rather
than a physical IR claim.

\paragraph{Class 44 ($a/N^{2}=1/2$, $R_{\text{bif}}=2/3$; leading-$N$ marginal).}
The $a$-maximal R-charges of class~44 equal the free-field value
$2/3$, giving $a/N^{2}=1/2$.  On each node the matter content exactly
saturates the leading-$N$ AF bound,
$\alpha^{(a)}=3-n_{\adj}^{(a)}-\tfrac{1}{2}(n_{S}+n_{\bar S}+n_{A}+n_{\bar A})
-\tfrac{k_{\text{tot}}}{2}=0$, so the one-loop beta function vanishes
at leading $N$.  As a result the boundary coefficients
$b_{0}^{(a)}(g_{b}^{\star})$ also vanish at leading $N$, and the RG
topology is determined by the sub-leading structure.  Class~$44$ is
not below unitarity and not nonSCFT; it is merely marginal at
leading~$N$.

\paragraph{nonSCFT umbrella: class 877 + 20 flat-direction theories.}
Class~$877$ collects quivers in which one of the two nodes has no
intrinsic matter ($n_{\adj}^{(a)}=n_{S}^{(a)}=n_{\bar S}^{(a)}=
n_{A}^{(a)}=n_{\bar A}^{(a)}=0$ for $a=1$ or $a=2$), so the entire
matter content of that node consists of bifundamentals to the other
node.  Of the 24 non-Veneziano empty-node enumerants, 15 have a flat
direction at the leading-$N$ $a$-maximisation and are excluded from
class counts; the remaining 9 admit a unique interior critical point
at $a/N^{2}=0$.  All 9 surviving representatives lie below the SQCD
conformal window at the empty node ($x^{(a)}<3/2$), so the gauge
dynamics on that node is confining/IR-free rather than
superconformal.  We therefore place the 9 class-$877$ representatives
in the \emph{nonSCFT} umbrella together with the 20 flat-direction
theories, and omit them from the phase-sign classification of
Section~\ref{sec:boundary}.  The 20 flat-direction theories carry no
class label in the database (\texttt{class\_id} is NULL) and are
included here on the same footing: their IR R-charges are not
uniquely determined, so no isolated SCFT fixed point can be
associated with the leading-$N$ analysis.

\paragraph{Summary.}
Classes $2,4,16,21,23$ have interior R-charge extrema within the
unitarity polytope.  Classes~$1$ and~$9$ have extrema on or below the
unitarity boundary but are retained in the boundary-coefficient
tables with a formal-Figure-5 caveat.  Class~$44$ is leading-$N$
marginal.  The nonSCFT umbrella — class~$877$ and the 20
flat-direction theories — is excluded from the boundary tables:
class~$877$ because it sits below the SQCD conformal window at its
empty node, and the flat-direction theories because their leading-$N$
$a$-max is not a unique local maximum.
