\subsection{Enumeration pipeline}
\label{sec:class-enumeration}

The two-node equal-rank sector is enumerated as follows.  We fix the
bifundamental multiplicity vector
\mbox{$k=(k_{++},k_{--},k_{+-},k_{-+})$} with total
$k_{\text{tot}}\le 4$ and, for each choice, scan over the allowed
tensor multiplicity tuples
$(n_{\adj}^{(a)},n_{S}^{(a)},n_{\bar S}^{(a)},n_{A}^{(a)},n_{\bar A}^{(a)})$
at each node~$a$ subject to
\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
  \item the leading-$N$ asymptotic-freedom condition
    \eqref{eq:AF-lead};
  \item the cubic-anomaly cancellation
    \eqref{eq:anomaly} at leading order in~$N$, with
    $\Nf^{(a),\pm}=0$ (non-Veneziano limit); and
  \item discrete symmetries interchanging the two nodes, node-0/1
    reflections, and (anti-)bifundamental flips, whose quotient
    defines the universality class.
\end{enumerate}
Each resulting representative is then subjected to symbolic
$a$-maximisation: the R-charge manifold is parameterised subject to the
gauge-anomaly-free constraints~\eqref{eq:anomaly-free}, the $a$-function
\eqref{eq:a-trial} is extremised symbolically, and only critical points
with a negative-definite Hessian are retained.  Theories sharing the
same $a/N^{2}$ to high precision are grouped into a universality
class.\footnote{Classes are labelled by their position in the global
$135$-class table of the full database; we inherit the original
indexing without relabelling.}

The five interacting classes in Table~\ref{tab:seven-classes} share
three salient features:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{$a=c$ to leading order.}  In each case the central
    charges coincide to leading order in $1/N$, $a/c=1$.  This is a
    strong signature: in general two-coupling SCFTs allow $a\neq c$
    at $\mathcal O(1)$ in $1/N$, but the extremal R-symmetry of these
    seven classes lies in a subgroup of the flavour symmetry that
    enforces $\Tr R^{3}=\Tr R$ at leading $N$.
  \item \textbf{Exact R-charges with square-root irrationals.}  The
    extremisation problem at each node reduces to a low-dimensional
    polynomial system in the node R-charges, whose solutions involve
    square roots of small integers ($\sqrt 5,\sqrt{10},\sqrt{13},
    \sqrt{17}$).
  \item \textbf{Positivity of all gauge-invariant operator
    dimensions.}  We verify that the a-maximising R-charges satisfy
    the unitarity bound $\Delta(\mathcal O)\ge 1$ for every
    gauge-invariant operator built from the matter content.  No
    accidental symmetries are required.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Degenerate classes.}  Class $1$ has $a/N^{2}=0$ with all
bifundamental R-charges vanishing; the a-maximum is at the corner of
the allowed region and the theory is free in the $1/N$ expansion (and
not unitary as an SCFT).  Class $44$ returns $R=2/3$ for every
bifundamental, giving the free-field value of $a/N^{2}=1/2$.  Both
degenerate classes are included in our tables for completeness but are
excluded from the RG-phase analysis below.

For instance, class $16$ has the bifundamental structure
$2\,(\fund,\fund)+(\afund,\afund)$ with tensors $A+2\bar A$ on node $0$
and $\bar A$ on node $1$.  Its a-maximum is
\begin{equation}
  a/N^{2}\bigl|_{16}\;=\;\tfrac{3}{32}+\tfrac{5\sqrt 5}{32}
  \;\simeq\;0.4431\,,
  \qquad
  R_{\text{bif}}\bigl|_{16}\;=\;\tfrac{1+\sqrt 5}{6}\;\simeq\;0.539\,,
\end{equation}
with the tensor and anti-tensor R-charges at node~$0$ degenerate,
$R_{A}^{(0)}=R_{\bar A}^{(0)}=(7-\sqrt 5)/6\simeq 0.794$.  This class
will play a central role in Section~\ref{sec:phases}.
